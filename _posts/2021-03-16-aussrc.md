So at the time of writing this post I worked for the CSIRO with a small group called the [AusSRC](https://aussrc.org/) that are aiming to establish a network of globally distributed post-processing facilities to assist scientists with the processing of O(100) PB of data from the SKA. It's one of the most interesting projects I've worked on up to this point in my career.

Here's an awesome photo of the OG design study team, after our first group strategy session.

![aussrc_team](https://www.dropbox.com/s/yu7wllkkrryhdc4/AusSRC.PNG?raw=1)

The aim is for there to be a network of these "Regional Centres", like us, distributed across the globe for locations with science teams that have an interest in SKA data. The AusSRC is the first to be established and funded, so we're playing a leading role in architectural and operational decisions by initiating this design study.

The description in the photo (which is a screenshot from another blog post) tells you more about the details. I wanted to share this screenshot too, which I think visually illustrates how far-reaching the impact of our work could be. Pretty cool stuff! Hopefully there will be more to come.
