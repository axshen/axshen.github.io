There's something nice about coastal towns like Albany, which reminds me of Hobart - it has a historic maritime feel to it. You can look out at the ocean and it's always beautiful, even when it's rainy and windy. And at this time of year there's always the hope that you'll spot a whale. In that way it's like Hobart too, I saw a shark on my way to work on the first day of my studentship program. No such luck this time with whales, sharks or seals. Just this menacing pirate boat (or wheat cargo ship, but probably not).

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/0m2e4qy1mme1fq6/bay.jpg?raw=1)

A group of us went down for the WA day long weekend. The weather was unpredictable this weekend so we had rain, wind and sun multiple times every day. Although it meant we couldn't really plan our activities, it did give us this wonderful double rainbow so I will forgive the weather gods this time. You could even say it was lucky - it's not often you see the end of the rainbow.

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/weyys6c71ehpihu/double_rainbow.jpg?raw=1)

Over the course of three days we'd go and do three different hikes. Naturally, I took photos of each so here they are.

### Day 1 - Bluff Knoll

I met up with the gang in Mount Barker which was the optimal mid-point between Albany, Margaret River (from which I was coming) and the Stirling Range National Park (our destination for the day). From there we went straight to Bluff Knoll - which is the highest peak in the Stirling Ranges. It was busy, as you'd expect on a public holiday weekend, so we all (7) crammed into one car and drove to the car park at the base of the mountain. No, not into a spacious 4WD with those uncomfortable back seats in the boot - this was a hatchback with a couple of people laying more horizontally than vertically to fit.

I was told that it was an easy climb and that there were stairs all the way up, and that mentality was reflected in my preparation. I chose to leave my sturdy hiking boots behind in the boot of the car in favour of my fashionable leather high-top converse shoes that I'm still wearing in. Terrible choice, literally blisters for days. It's not the most technical climb in the National Park sure, but I will definitely be wearing good shoes in the future.

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/9jvq1utxf2744qu/bird.jpg?raw=1)

It was a cloudy day, and from the base we could not see the summit. There were still nice lookouts along the way up. There were moments at the summit where the clouds would disappear temporarily and reveal the rolling hills of the mountain range, but I think I liked the photos from half-way up more. 

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/fym0s39o2do5a50/stirling_ranges.jpg?raw=1)

I've been to Albany a few times now, and the time that I seem to remember best is when I visited my friend Ben while he was studying there. Drinks and half-price Peruvian Parmies at Six Senses on Sunday. We went back there after the hike and it was as good as I remember it.

### Day 2 - Castle Rock

We were very undecided on what to do on the second day. A series of short hikes was picked in favour of a section between camps of the Bibbulmun Track, I guess because we were tired from something. Took it easy in the morning breathing in the fumes from the racecars here for an annual classic car race. The unpredictable weather along the coast didn't help either. We started off with Castle Rock, which is a gentle and short (hour-long) hike to a rock formation that resembles a castle. But not really lol, it was just a bunch of large boulders. I had my hiking shoes this time, so despite the calf fatigue from the previous day this climb felt easy in comparison.

I really enjoyed this hike. It was probably that we took our time playing amongst the large rocks at the end, but the great conversations, snacks and comfortable nature probably all contributed. The trees along the trail were all relatively high, so you couldn't see the landscape is the distance. So on this hike I spent more time with my camera on its macro setting.

After this hike we got fish and chips from Ocean & Paddock - known for their famous South Carolina BBQ sauce.

### Day 3 - Bald Head

We were rushed for time on the last day, trying to get back to Perth for around 5pm. That meant leaving Albany by mid-day. After checking out, we made our way, in the two cars we'd be driving back to Perth in, to Bald Head for one final hike. The Bald Head hike is supposed to take four hours, so instead of committing to the whole hike our plan was to just turn around when necessary. My car finished breakfast quickly and thought it would take the others much longer to eat (they'd opted to eat at a dine-in cafe and not drive-through Maccas), so we detoured past The Blowholes on the way to Bald Head. The Blowholes were pretty cool, but a little underwhelming on this occasion.

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/gy9sztzsw0i92wm/blowholes.jpg?raw=1)

There is no reception at The Blowholes, so after getting back on the road our notifications showed a (large) number of missed calls from the other group. Oops. We had kept the others waiting for at least 20 minutes. Terrible form. We got to Bald Head and everyone was fine. At the start of the hike there is a series of steep walks on a narrow concrete boardwalk surrounded by high trees. It was not after the boardwalk ended that the trees cleared and you could see the bay and ocean on either side of the trail. It reminded me a lot of this lookout on Bruny Island. I kept an eye out on both sides for whales.

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/shxe0q2xl0sfy29/bald_head.jpg?raw=1)

![img](https://www.dropbox.com/s/i1s28z4wpml4hmw/cliff.jpg?raw=1)

I felt that this walk was even easier than those of the previous two days. The sand absorbed a lot of the force from our steps as we descended so it was nice on our failing knees. It was flat too, and therefore easy to run along. I would fall back for photos and run back up to the group to catch up without any difficulties. At Bluff Knoll they would have had to stop and wait. But it was worth it to capture some nice photos, like the one below, of my good friends.
