We have two public holidays around Easter which leads to a 4-day weekend. We decided to go camping in Jurien Bay. To beat the holiday traffic (made worse by COVID) I took the Thursday off too. So good - 5 day weekend! It was also probably important to beat the crowd because we didn't book anywhere. Even though they have an overflow camping spot, there was still the risk this time that we weren't able to find a spot. Anyway (spoilers) that wasn't a problem after all. We got there, set up shade and enjoyed some nice beers while the rest of Perth was finishing off work for their long weekend.

![camp_site](https://www.dropbox.com/s/0tvcvvytu2z34b8/tent.jpeg?raw=1)

I never thought there was much to do in Jurien Bay. There's a nice beach there, a sporting oval and some small shops but not much else aside from that. Maybe that's what you need for a weekend away.

On our first day we went to Sandy Cape, which also happens to be where Google Maps thinks the Jurien Bay Marine Park is. We've been here before - I think we did some stand-up paddleboarding and sandboarding here last time. I also remember swimming across the bay - terrifying. We planned to snorkel here, but there were heaps of jellyfish and the water was murky. Gave it a try anyway and got stung on the face. Much sad. Gave it a try again in a different corner of the bay and got knocked around by the waves. Just not the day for it. That's okay - it was a great day for frisbee and chills on the beach.

![nice_catch_jake](https://www.dropbox.com/s/pcvsxie7uttc48d/frisbee.jpeg?raw=1)

We went for a walk after that to the North side of the bay and found this incredible snorkelling spot with calm water and reef. Will have to remember to go there next time!

![north_sandy_cape](https://www.dropbox.com/s/vvdfd2uk0aptij0/group.jpeg?raw=1)

It was a fun evening too - had some hot cross buns (as you do) with some of Tekky's home made butter. Fish and chips at the camp site, more beer and card games ([exploding kittens](https://www.explodingkittens.com/) if you must know).

The next day we drove down to Lancelin. It was overcast and lightly raining sporadically. Sort of a strange vibe but there was some good swell. So we spend the whole afternoon chilling by the beach on our picnic mat. Jake and I went for a surf. 

![surf_board](https://www.dropbox.com/s/tsuv245dokcn85y/surf.jpeg?raw=1)

We got the greasiest burger there and made our way back home. Thanks for the great holiday everyone :)
