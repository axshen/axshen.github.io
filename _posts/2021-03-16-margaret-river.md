My first job after graduating from UWA was at a consulting firm called [Accenture](https://www.accenture.com/au-en). In my time there I met so many great people - some who I now consider to be very close friends. We had a great Analyst group (basically graduates) and my first year there we organised our first road trip down to WA's best wine region ([Margaret River](https://www.margaretriver.com/)). We've done that just about every year since (with the exception of 2020 - fuck you COVID).

Each road trip follows the tried-and-tested formula from the first one. We get a nice big house down south (it's beautiful every time) for the weekend. We book bus to take us to a bunch of wineries/breweries in the region for the day. Below is one of us at [Swings and Roundabouts](https://www.swings.com.au/). It might even be the same driver every time. We drink on both nights, and take it a little easier on Sunday as we make our way back home. On the road trip, we play [odds](https://www.wikihow.com/Play-What-Are-the-Odds) and get each other to do silly stuff. And we're singing on the bus after about 3pm when we're all completely wrecked.

![the gang](https://www.dropbox.com/s/lig9iinbjaov8zv/a820f45b-f364-4499-ac6f-21f15c6ff7ac.jpeg?raw=1)

This time I got up earlier both mornings because I was sensible and drank water at various points during the day (and ramped that up in the evening - yay for the experience that comes with age). The first morning I was able to get up and go to [Hamelin Bay](https://www.westernaustralia.com/en/Attraction/Hamelin_Bay/56b268c6aeeeaaf773cfb6a8) with a couple of friends for a morning snorkel. The beach is known for its resident sting rays, which are supposed to be there all the time. The last two times I went, I wasn't able to see them. 

I got lucky this time though! 

![sting ray](https://www.dropbox.com/s/6ne6o7kgh7lm3hy/IMG_0571.jpeg?raw=1)

The second day was very active. I've always wanted to go snorkelling at a rock pool called the Aquarium near Yallingup. Everyone was keen so we went there after our group brunch. Here's a photo - it really doesn't even begin to do the place any justice (it's so much more beautiful). There's a lot of fish, some beautiful coral/sea grass, it's sheltered from the waves and feels like a very safe place to swim. I saw some cuttlefish! It's probably up to 5m deep, so it's a great spot for freediving. Hopefully next time I'll have a dive camera to use.

![the aquarium](https://www.dropbox.com/s/uf2bbrkrykua5it/IMG_0665.jpeg?raw=1)

We planned to chill out there for a couple of hours and go to one of the nicest restaurants in the South West WA region. BUT... we dropped our car key in the rocks before the pool! So yeah, had to cancel the booking and scramble to find a locksmith. It was all fine in the end - we played [Mafia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mafia_%28party_game%29) sitting in the shade of our ute, bought some food at a nearby general store and had a nice picnic on the rocks. Pretty wholesome after all :)
