I got early this *very* cold Saturday morning in the middle of Makuru to go for a snorkel with some buds at the Omeo Shipwreck. In the warmer weather Omeo is very busy and I've been told it's best to go early to avoid the crowds and to ensure the visibility is decent. Turns out it's not so much an issue at this time of year. When we arrived there was maybe 2-3 groups of people out in the water, all in wetsuits and some going for a dive around the [Coogee Maritime Trail](https://www.cockburn.wa.gov.au/Recreation-and-Attractions/Trails-Tracks-and-Lookouts/Coogee-Maritime-Trail). 

The air temperature this morning was just below 10 degrees. On the drive over, wearing layers, uggs and in the warm car air-conditioning, we discussed how the water temperature is *much* warmer than the air temperature. You know, stuff about how the water here is carried by the Leeuwin down from the warm tropics. And how the rashie top that Jerry brought would probably be fine. Lol.

![kooper](https://www.dropbox.com/s/2bt9qt4bzmf4h53/kooper.jpg?raw=1)

The water was damn cold. As we got in the clouds decided that we needed to be punished for our terrible decisions as they blocked all sunlight and source of natural warmth. I was only slighly better prepared than Jerry. I brought a fleece-lined vest with a hood that I wore over the top of my long-sleeve rashie. I have a 5mm wetsuit at home, so it's the second time recently that I've sufferred the elements unnecessarily (Bluff Knoll was the other time). It was all still very exciting - it was the first time I was able to try out the underwater camera since I bought it.

![coral](https://www.dropbox.com/s/7l1xhn8ltru8udx/coral.jpg?raw=1)

It was great fun trying to take photos underwater. More challenging than I thought originally, because you're trying to maintain buoyancy and hold your breath while stabilising the camera to focus on the subject of the photo. A good housing and those two-handed mount things would really help. Fish don't just let you take photos of them either, even though they're curious about you. I was chasing this one for a while before getting close enough to take this photo. This fish is called an Old Wife, and there were schools of them in this marine park.

![old-wife-fish](https://www.dropbox.com/s/erx0an2y8qplfs0/old-wife-fish.jpg?raw=1)

![fish-group](https://www.dropbox.com/s/cxl66kuywkyvhyy/fish-group.jpg?raw=1)

There's also a little Stripey fish in the corner, and another fish that I haven't been able to identify. It's probably a Skipjack Trevally. Once you snorkel past the wreck there's a bunch of artificial reefs that we didn't get to explore. I was chasing this Crested Morwong in and out of this tiny cave that we human overlords created for this little fishy. It seemed to be enjoying itself.

![artificial-reef-cave](https://www.dropbox.com/s/1vkd3hb6hloqi9l/fishy.jpg?raw=1)
