There's a nice waterfall in Lesmurdie on the Darling Range enscarpment (hills) with some relatively easy bush walking trails. Our Cheeky Hiking Club decided it would be good fun to go there for a sunset hike this weekend. It was also one of the first opportunities to play with a [new camera](https://olympus-imaging.com.au/product/compact/tg6/index.html) I bought mainly for underwater photography (more to come!). 

This is the view from the start of the trail. It looks across the city and down the waterfall. The trail goes down along the waterfall, across it and back up along the waterfall on the other side. The flow was not particularly strong on this day unfortunately - we'll have to come back after bigger rains in winter.

![falls](https://www.dropbox.com/s/x8e0dcovhvckf5y/falls.jpg?raw=1)

I really liked how this photo turned out. I took all of these photos in RAW format so I could edit them after and it makes such a big difference. Not that the photo was bad originally - I liked it enough to pick it for editing, but the detail and colour along the horizon which gives this photo depth (you can even see the city) was completely enabled by post-processing. Still early days in my photography journey but it's cool to see how big a difference photo editing makes.

Anyway, towards the end of the trail there's a clearing to look out over the city. It took us about an hour to walk here from the starting point. We were just in time for sunset so I was able to take this nice photo of Perth.

![perth](https://www.dropbox.com/s/58my4ckowe6u4r3/perth.jpg?raw=1)

We brought some nibbles for the top, had nice chats and just relaxed until just after dark on some smooth af rocks.
