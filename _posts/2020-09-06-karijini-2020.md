## Sept 2020

My first time travelling to Karijini! It's a beautiful National Park in the North of WA, known for the pretty, clear and blue fresh water pools at the bottom of red gorges. It's a long drive up from Perth (11-13 hours) so getting there takes a couple of days depending on how much you want to enjoy the journey up. We stayed at the Dales campgrounds for the week. 

I could write for a while describing each day in detail (and maybe I will regret not doing this when I start to forget things) but all I can be bothered doing at the moment is sharing some key thoughts about the holiday. Here they are:

* Fern Pool is one of the most beautiful places I've ever been to. Was definitely worth checking out a couple of times on the trip, and I'd consider coming back to Karijini (after a couple of years) just for this place. I've got a photo below of the pool.
* Having dingoes at the camp sites is pretty awesome. You hear them howling at the moon at night, and if you're lucky, hear them running through bushes around the site. I'm sure not everyone feels this way.
* 5 days was just about the perfect amount of time to be camping there. You need to pack everything (food, water, cleaning stuff) so by the end of it I was preeeetty keen for good food, a warm shower and sleep in your cushy bed.
* The tallest mountains in WA are not in the [Stirling Ranges](https://parks.dpaw.wa.gov.au/park/stirling-range) but in the Pilbara region! Mt Meharry #1, Mt Bruce #2. Bluff Knoll? Nowhere near the top of this list.

Here's some of the favourite photos I took of the trip:

### Got swag

Although this photo wasn't taken in the Karijini National Park, it's pretty much the sleeping arrangement we had for the entire week. It was also my first time in a swag, and it was great! It's quick to set up and pack up and more comfortable than any tent/mattress combination I've tried. This photo was taken at a campsite near Meekatharra, where we stopped both on the way up and back. 

![our_bed](https://www.dropbox.com/s/awrobedml1a3uip/camp.JPG?raw=1)

### The gorges

All of the gorges are beautiful in their own way, but this was my second favourite. Hamersley Gorge, but on the side that isn't where Spa Pool is. Spa Pool is one of the most popular Instagram spots, but there's really not that much to do once you've taken that photo. On this side the water is deep and clear, and there's a long swim through the rock. I tried touching the ground here but it was pretty deep. Would have been pretty bad if I'd dropped my phone :S 

![gorge](https://www.dropbox.com/s/toa0mslschfnr3f/gorge.jpg?raw=1)

### Hike

So to get to the pools in any of the gorges you have to do a hecking tone of hiking up and down rocks. We wanted to do more so on the way out of the National Park we thought we'd try go for a hike up the highest peak in WA. Turns out Mt Meharry is a tough hike with even tougher roads leading up to it, so that wasn't an option given our time constraints. The 2nd highest peak was much nicer. So we woke up super early and walked up Mt Bruce that instead. 

![mt_bruce](https://www.dropbox.com/s/1j5qz0lsnjtavkt/mt_bruce.jpg?raw=1)

### And finally, my favourite

The prettiest place in the whole park - Fern Pool. We got there a bit later in the day and the sun was low on the horizon, pretty much about to set. It's a dark blue pool surrounded by green trees and ferns, except for this waterfall on the far side. It was also (unfortunately) the last pool we'd visit on our trip. And it was my birthday. We swam over and chilled underneath the waterfall for a while.
